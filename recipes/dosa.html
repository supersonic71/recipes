Dosa recipe

Description

A dosa is a thin batter-based dish (usually crispy) originating from South India,
made from a fermented batter predominantly consisting of lentils and rice.
Its main ingredients are rice and black gram, ground together in a fine,
smooth batter with a dash of salt, then fermented. Dosas are a common dish in
South Indian cuisine, but now have been popular all over the Indian subcontinent.

Dosas are served hot along with chutney by tradition and sambar in recent times.
Chutney is a fine paste made up of groundnut, coconut and lentil.

Ingredients

  • 3/4 cup Parboiled Rice (idli-dosa rice)
  • 3/4 cup Regular Rice
  • 1/4 teaspoon Fenugreek Seeds

Recipe

  1. Rinse both types of rice (regular rice and parboiled rice) together in water for 3-4 times.
  2. Rinse urad dal and chana dal together in water.
  3. Add water as needed and grind until smooth and fluffy batter.
  4. Add water as needed and grind until smooth texture.
