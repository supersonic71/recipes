Idli recipe

Description

Idli or idly are a type of savoury rice cake, originating from the Indian subcontinent,
popular as breakfast foods in Southern India and in Sri Lanka. The cakes are made
by steaming a batter consisting of fermented black lentils (de-husked) and rice.
The fermentation process breaks down the starches so that they are more readily
metabolised by the body.

Idli has several variations, including rava idli, which is made from semolina.
Regional variants include sanna of Konkan.

Ingredients

  • 2 1/2 cup basmati rice
  • 1 1/2 cup urad dal
  • 5 tablespoon sesame oil

Recipe

  1. Soak the rice & dal and grind into a paste & combine
  2. Let the batter ferment, then add salt
  3. Transfer the idli batter in the idli stand
  4. Wait for the utensil to cool a bit before scooping the idlis out
